# Emergency Protocols for Trekkers

## Before You Go
- Share your detailed itinerary with at least two people not on the trek
- Register with the local forest department or police station at the trailhead
- Save offline maps of the entire route (Google Maps, OsmAnd, or AllTrails)
- Ensure your phone is fully charged and carry a power bank (20,000+ mAh)

## Essential Emergency Gear
- **Satellite communicator**: Garmin inReach Mini 2 or ZOLEO (works where there is no cell signal)
- **Whistle**: Three blasts = universal distress signal
- **Signal mirror**: For helicopter rescue visibility
- **Headlamp with fresh batteries**: Essential if evacuation happens at night
- **Emergency bivvy or space blanket**: Weighs under 100g, can save your life in hypothermia

## SOS Procedures
1. **Stay calm** -- panic causes poor decisions
2. **Assess the situation**: Is the injured person stable? Can they walk?
3. **Administer first aid** immediately (stop bleeding, stabilise fractures, treat for shock)
4. **Call for help**: Try mobile phone first. If no signal, use satellite communicator
5. **Do not move** a person with suspected spinal injury unless in immediate danger
6. **Mark your location**: Use GPS coordinates, nearby landmarks, and altitude

## Emergency Numbers
- **National Emergency**: 112 (works even without SIM)
- **Ambulance**: 108 (free in most states)
- **NDRF (National Disaster Response Force)**: 011-24363260
- **Uttarakhand SDRF**: 1070
- **Himachal Pradesh Emergency**: 01177-2622763
- **Jammu & Kashmir Emergency Operations**: 0194-2440020

## Evacuation Guidelines
- **Walking evacuation**: If the person can walk with support, descend to the nearest road head
- **Stretcher evacuation**: Improvise with trekking poles and a sleeping bag if needed. Requires 4-6 people rotating
- **Helicopter evacuation**: Only for life-threatening situations. Costs Rs 1-3 lakh+. Requires clear weather and a flat landing zone. Signal with bright clothing or a mirror

## When to Abort a Trek
- Any participant shows signs of HACE or HAPE
- Weather deteriorates rapidly with no shelter available
- Trail becomes impassable due to landslides or flooding
- Group is significantly behind schedule with insufficient daylight
- Anyone sustains an injury preventing safe continuation
