# Leave No Trace: Responsible Trekking in India

## Why It Matters
Popular Indian trails like Triund, Kedarkantha, and Roopkund face severe degradation from littering and irresponsible camping. Leave No Trace (LNT) principles help preserve these trails for future generations.

## The 7 LNT Principles (Adapted for Indian Trails)

### 1. Plan Ahead and Prepare
- Research trail conditions and regulations before you go
- Carry sufficient food so you do not depend on trail-side vendors in fragile ecosystems
- Check if permits are required (many Himalayan treks need forest department permits)
- Travel in small groups -- large groups cause more trail damage

### 2. Travel and Camp on Durable Surfaces
- Stay on marked trails -- do not create shortcuts through meadows (bugyal)
- Camp only at designated campsites
- Avoid walking on fragile alpine vegetation above the tree line

### 3. Dispose of Waste Properly
- **Pack it in, pack it out**: Carry ALL waste back, including food scraps, wrappers, and cigarette butts
- Use a dedicated trash bag and weigh it before and after to ensure nothing is left
- For human waste: dig a cathole 6-8 inches deep, at least 200 feet from water sources. Cover and disguise it
- Carry a ziplock bag for used toilet paper -- do not burn or bury it

### 4. Leave What You Find
- Do not pick flowers, remove stones, or disturb historical/cultural artefacts
- Do not carve names on trees or rocks
- Leave prayer flags and religious markers undisturbed

### 5. Minimise Campfire Impact
- Use a camping stove instead of making fires (many Indian alpine zones have scarce firewood)
- If fires are permitted, use existing fire rings and burn only small sticks collected from the ground
- Ensure fires are completely extinguished -- drown with water, stir ashes, feel with hand

### 6. Respect Wildlife
- Observe animals from a distance -- never feed them
- Store food in sealed containers (especially important in bear country around Himachal and Uttarakhand)
- Do not disturb nesting birds or block animal trails

### 7. Be Considerate of Other Visitors
- Keep noise levels low, especially at campsites after 9 PM
- Yield to uphill hikers on narrow trails
- Share trail information with fellow trekkers
- Play music only through earphones, never on speakers
