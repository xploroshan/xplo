# Fitness Preparation for Trekking

## Why Prepare?
Trekking in the Himalayas or Western Ghats demands sustained effort over hours. A 4-week training plan significantly reduces injury risk and makes the experience enjoyable rather than a survival exercise.

## 4-Week Training Plan

### Week 1-2: Build Base Fitness
- **Walking**: 5 km daily at a brisk pace (under 50 minutes)
- **Stair climbing**: 15-20 floors daily (find a building or use a stair machine)
- **Squats**: 3 sets of 15 bodyweight squats
- **Lunges**: 3 sets of 12 per leg
- **Core**: 2-minute plank hold, 3 sets

### Week 3: Increase Intensity
- **Walking with load**: 8 km with a 5-8 kg backpack
- **Stair climbing**: 25-30 floors with backpack
- **Step-ups**: 3 sets of 15 per leg on a knee-height bench
- **Calf raises**: 3 sets of 20 (critical for descents)
- **Practice hike**: One 10-12 km hike on a weekend with moderate elevation

### Week 4: Simulate Trek Conditions
- **Back-to-back hikes**: Two consecutive days of 8-10 km walks
- **Long hike**: One 15 km hike with your actual trekking backpack (fully loaded)
- **Cardio**: 30-minute jog or cycling session, 3 times
- **Stretching**: 15 minutes daily focusing on hamstrings, quads, hip flexors

## Cardio Benchmarks
Before attempting a moderate Himalayan trek (like Kedarkantha or Hampta Pass):
- Walk 10 km in under 2 hours on flat terrain
- Climb 30 floors without stopping
- Jog 5 km comfortably

## Key Tips
- Start training at least 4 weeks before the trek -- do not cram
- Train in the shoes you will trek in to break them in
- Hydrate properly during training, simulating trail water intake
- If you have knee issues, consult a physiotherapist and invest in quality trekking poles
- Do NOT skip rest days -- muscles strengthen during recovery, not during exercise
