# Weather Preparation for Campers

## Reading Weather Signs
When you are hours from civilisation, basic weather reading can keep you safe.

**Signs of Incoming Bad Weather**:
- Clouds building rapidly from the west, especially towering cumulonimbus
- Sudden drop in temperature or change in wind direction
- Increasing wind speed throughout the day
- Halo around the sun or moon (indicates moisture in upper atmosphere)
- Birds flying low and insects becoming more active

**Signs of Clearing Weather**:
- Morning fog that lifts by mid-morning
- Steady barometric pressure (if you carry a watch with barometer)
- Winds shifting to come from the west/northwest

## Shelter Priorities
When severe weather threatens, your priorities are:
1. **Get off exposed ridges and peaks** -- lightning strikes the highest point
2. **Seek natural shelter** -- dense forest, rock overhangs, valleys
3. **Secure your tent** -- re-tension all guylines, add extra stakes, point the narrow end into the wind
4. **Waterproof your gear** -- pack everything in dry bags or trash bags inside your backpack

## Lightning Safety
Lightning kills more people in India than any other natural disaster. Follow the 30-30 rule:
- If the time between flash and thunder is less than 30 seconds, seek shelter immediately
- Wait 30 minutes after the last flash before resuming activity

**If Caught in the Open**:
- Move away from tall trees, metal objects, and water bodies
- Do NOT lie flat -- crouch on the balls of your feet with feet together, head tucked, ears covered
- Spread the group out (at least 5 metres apart) to reduce multiple casualties
- Drop metal-frame backpacks and trekking poles at a distance

## Rain Fly Setup
- The rain fly should be taut with no wrinkles -- sagging collects water pools that eventually leak
- Ensure the fly extends past the tent body on all sides
- Guylines should be tensioned at 45 degrees from the tent
- In heavy rain, dig a shallow drainage channel around the tent (only if on soil, not meadow)
- Angle the vestibule opening downhill so water runs away from the door

## Wind Protection
- Never camp on exposed ridgelines if high winds are forecasted
- Use natural windbreaks: rock walls, dense bushes, terrain dips
- Double-stake the windward side of your tent
- Store all loose gear inside the tent or vestibule -- wind scatters items quickly
- In extreme wind, lower your tent profile by removing the fly and weighting the tent with gear inside
