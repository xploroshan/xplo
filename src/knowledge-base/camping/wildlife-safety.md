# Wildlife Safety While Camping

## General Principles
India's wilderness hosts diverse and sometimes dangerous wildlife. Respect their habitat and take precautions to avoid encounters.

## Bear Safety (Himalayan Black Bear & Brown Bear)
Bears are present across Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, J&K, and Sikkim.

**Prevention**:
- Make noise while hiking to avoid surprising bears
- Cook and store food at least 100 metres from your tent
- Use bear canisters or hang food bags 4 metres high on a branch, 2 metres from the trunk
- Never keep food, toothpaste, or scented items in your tent

**If You Encounter a Bear**:
- Do NOT run -- bears can outrun humans
- Back away slowly while speaking in a calm, low voice
- Make yourself appear larger -- raise your arms, open your jacket wide
- If a black bear attacks: fight back aggressively (eyes, nose, face)
- If a brown bear attacks: play dead, lying face down, hands behind your neck, legs spread to resist being flipped

## Snake Awareness
India has the "Big Four" venomous snakes: Cobra, Krait, Russell's Viper, and Saw-scaled Viper.

**Prevention**:
- Watch where you step, especially on rocky trails and near water
- Shake out boots and sleeping bags before use
- Use a headlamp when walking at night
- Keep tent zipped at all times

**If Bitten**:
- Stay calm and immobilise the bitten limb
- Remove rings and watches (swelling will occur)
- Do NOT apply a tourniquet, cut the wound, or try to suck venom
- Get to the nearest hospital immediately -- antivenom is the only effective treatment
- Note the snake's appearance if possible for identification

## Insect Protection
- Apply DEET-based repellent (20-30% concentration) to exposed skin
- Treat clothing and tent mesh with permethrin spray
- Sleep under a mosquito net in lower-altitude jungle camps
- Check for ticks after hiking through tall grass -- remove with fine-tipped tweezers using steady, upward pressure
- Carry antihistamines for allergic reactions to stings

## Food Storage Rules
- Never leave food unattended at camp
- Clean up all food scraps and cooking grease immediately
- Dispose of dishwater at least 100 metres from camp, strained of food particles
- In monkey-prone areas (Western Ghats), secure all bags -- monkeys are skilled at opening zips
