# Campsite Setup Guide

## Choosing Your Spot
The right campsite can mean the difference between a good night's sleep and a miserable experience. Follow these guidelines:

### Location Checklist
- **Flat ground**: Look for the flattest area available. Even a slight slope means you will slide in your sleep
- **Drainage**: Avoid depressions or dry riverbeds -- they flood in rain. Look for slightly elevated ground
- **Wind protection**: Set up behind natural windbreaks like boulders or tree lines. Position your tent door away from prevailing wind
- **Water proximity**: Camp within 200 metres of a water source but NEVER right next to it (wildlife visits water at night, and flash floods are a risk)
- **Sun exposure**: In winter, pick east-facing spots for morning sun warmth. In summer, prioritise shade

## Ground Preparation
1. Clear rocks, sticks, and pinecones from your tent footprint
2. Lay down a groundsheet or footprint slightly smaller than your tent floor
3. If the ground is soft, use full-length stakes. For rocky ground, use heavier nail-style stakes or weigh down guylines with rocks

## Tent Placement
- Align your tent lengthwise with the wind direction so wind flows over the narrow profile
- Stake all corners first, then attach guylines for stability
- Ensure the rain fly is taut with no sagging -- pooled water will eventually seep through
- Leave the vestibule accessible for gear storage and emergency exits

## Securing Stakes
- Drive stakes at a 45-degree angle away from the tent
- In sandy soil, bury stakes horizontally (deadman anchor technique)
- In snow, pack snow around buried stakes or use snow stakes
- Carry at least 2-3 extra stakes -- they bend and break

## Camp Kitchen
- Set up cooking area at least 50 metres downwind from your tent
- Store food in sealed containers away from your sleeping area (critical in bear country)
- Never cook inside your tent -- carbon monoxide from stoves is lethal in enclosed spaces
- Hang food bags from a tree branch at least 3 metres high in wildlife areas

## Breaking Camp
- Pack everything and inspect the site thoroughly
- Fill in any holes you dug
- Scatter any collected firewood ashes
- Leave the site looking as if no one was ever there
