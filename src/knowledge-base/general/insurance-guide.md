# Adventure Sports and Travel Insurance Guide

## Why You Need It
Medical evacuation from a remote trekking location can cost Rs 1-5 lakh. A broken bone abroad or a helicopter rescue without insurance can be financially devastating. Adventure insurance is not optional -- it is essential.

## What Standard Travel Insurance Covers
Most basic travel insurance policies cover:
- Medical emergencies and hospitalisation
- Trip cancellation or interruption
- Baggage loss or delay
- Personal accident (death and disability)

## What Standard Policies Typically EXCLUDE
- **Adventure sports and activities** (the exact reason you need specialised cover)
- Pre-existing medical conditions
- Injuries under the influence of alcohol
- Reckless behaviour or ignoring safety guidelines
- Activities at extreme altitudes (often above 4,000-6,000 m)
- Professional or competitive sports

## Adventure-Specific Coverage to Look For
When buying insurance for outdoor activities, ensure the policy explicitly covers:
- **Trekking** (check altitude limit -- many cap at 4,000 m or 6,000 m)
- **White water rafting** (check grade limit)
- **Cycling and mountain biking**
- **Scuba diving** (check depth limit)
- **Motorcycle touring** (many policies exclude two-wheelers)
- **Search and rescue / helicopter evacuation**
- **Emergency medical evacuation** to the nearest suitable hospital

## Tips for Buying
- Read the **fine print** -- check the activity list, altitude limits, and exclusion clauses
- Declare any pre-existing medical conditions honestly
- Buy coverage **before** the trip starts (not after reaching the destination)
- Keep the policy document, claim helpline number, and emergency assistance number accessible offline
- Take photos of your gear (with serial numbers) before departure for baggage claims
- Group organisers should ensure participants have individual coverage or arrange group policies

## Making a Claim
- **Report immediately**: Call the insurance helpline as soon as the incident occurs
- Collect all documentation: hospital bills, FIR (if theft/accident), doctor's reports, receipts
- Take photos of injuries, damaged gear, and the incident scene
- File the claim within the policy's stipulated time (usually 30 days)
- Keep originals of all documents -- insurers typically require them
