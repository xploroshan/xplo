# Emergency Contacts for Outdoor Adventures in India

## National Emergency Numbers
- **112** -- Unified emergency number (works for police, fire, ambulance). Works even without SIM card or network balance
- **108** -- Ambulance service (free in most states, government-operated)
- **100** -- Police
- **101** -- Fire service
- **1033** -- National Highway Helpline (for highway accidents and assistance)
- **1078** -- Disaster Management helpline

## Disaster and Rescue
- **NDRF (National Disaster Response Force)**: 011-24363260, 9711077372
- **Indian Coast Guard**: 1554 (for coastal and maritime emergencies)

## Insurance and Assistance
- Note your travel insurance policy number and 24-hour helpline before departure
- Most international travel insurance providers have India toll-free numbers
- Keep a printed card with your blood group, allergies, emergency contact, and insurance details in your wallet and backpack

## Tips
- Save these numbers on your phone before every trip
- Share your itinerary and these numbers with your emergency contact
- In remote areas without mobile signal, try climbing to higher ground for reception
- Consider carrying a satellite communicator (Garmin inReach, ZOLEO) for areas with zero mobile coverage
