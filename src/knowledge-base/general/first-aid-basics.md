# First Aid Basics for Outdoor Adventures

## RICE Method (For Sprains and Strains)
- **R**est: Stop activity immediately. Do not walk on a sprained ankle
- **I**ce: Apply cold pack or ice wrapped in cloth for 15-20 minutes every 2 hours. Never apply ice directly to skin
- **C**ompression: Wrap with crepe bandage. Firm but not tight -- check that toes/fingers stay warm and pink
- **E**levation: Raise the injured limb above heart level to reduce swelling

## Wound Cleaning
1. Wash your hands or wear gloves
2. Rinse the wound with clean water (not antiseptic) to remove dirt and debris
3. Apply pressure with clean gauze if bleeding. Maintain pressure for 10-15 minutes
4. Apply povidone-iodine (Betadine) around the wound edges
5. Cover with sterile gauze and secure with medical tape
6. **Seek medical help if**: wound is deep, does not stop bleeding, shows signs of infection (redness spreading, pus, fever)

## Snake Bite First Response
1. **Stay calm** -- most Indian snake bites are from non-venomous species
2. Keep the victim still and calm. Movement increases venom spread
3. Immobilise the bitten limb with a splint, keeping it at or below heart level
4. Remove rings, watches, tight clothing near the bite (swelling will occur)
5. **Do NOT**: Apply tourniquet, cut the wound, suck the venom, apply ice
6. Note the time of the bite and describe the snake if possible
7. Get to the nearest hospital with antivenom facility IMMEDIATELY

## Heat Stroke
**Symptoms**: Body temperature above 40C, confusion, hot dry skin (no sweating), rapid pulse, headache.

**Treatment**:
1. Move to shade or cool area immediately
2. Remove excess clothing
3. Cool aggressively: pour water over body, fan the person, apply ice packs to neck, armpits, and groin
4. Give cool water to drink if conscious
5. This is a medical emergency -- call 108 immediately

## Hypothermia
**Symptoms**: Shivering, confusion, slurred speech, drowsiness, loss of coordination.

**Treatment**:
1. Move to shelter, out of wind and rain
2. Remove wet clothing, replace with dry layers
3. Wrap in sleeping bag with warm water bottles at core areas (chest, neck, groin)
4. Give warm, sweet drinks (NOT alcohol)
5. Do NOT rub extremities -- this can cause cardiac arrest
6. Severe hypothermia requires hospital treatment

## CPR Basics (Hands-Only)
1. Check if the person is responsive -- tap shoulders and shout
2. Call 112 or 108 immediately
3. Place the heel of one hand on the centre of the chest, other hand on top
4. Push hard and fast -- 5-6 cm depth, 100-120 compressions per minute
5. Continue until help arrives or the person recovers
6. If trained and willing, give 2 rescue breaths after every 30 compressions
