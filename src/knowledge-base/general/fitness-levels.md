# Fitness Level Definitions for Outdoor Activities

## Why Fitness Levels Matter
Accurate fitness self-assessment prevents dangerous situations. Joining an advanced trek when you are a beginner risks your safety and the group's. Be honest about your current level and train up gradually.

## Beginner
**Physical Capability**: Can walk up to 5 km on flat terrain without significant fatigue.

**Suitable Activities**:
- Short nature walks and easy trails (under 5 km)
- Flat cycling rides (under 20 km)
- Grade I-II white water rafting (operator does most of the work)
- Guided group motorcycle rides on paved roads
- 5K fun runs and walking events
- Flatwater kayaking and SUP

**Typical Profile**: Sedentary to lightly active lifestyle. No regular exercise routine. May be starting a fitness journey.

**Recommendation**: Start with beginner-tagged events. Build a base fitness of 4-6 weeks before attempting intermediate activities.

## Intermediate
**Physical Capability**: Can walk 5-15 km with moderate elevation gain (up to 500 m). Comfortable with sustained physical effort for 4-6 hours.

**Suitable Activities**:
- Moderate treks (Triund, Kheerganga, Harishchandragad -- 10-15 km, up to 3,000 m altitude)
- Cycling rides of 30-60 km with moderate hills
- Grade III white water rafting
- Half-day or full-day motorcycle rides on mixed terrain
- 10K and half marathon running events
- Multi-day camping trips

**Typical Profile**: Exercises 2-3 times per week. Can jog 5 km. Has done a few outdoor activities before.

## Advanced
**Physical Capability**: Can sustain 15+ km of hiking with significant elevation gain (1,000+ m). Comfortable at high altitude (up to 4,500 m) and in variable weather.

**Suitable Activities**:
- Challenging treks (Kedarkantha, Hampta Pass, Roopkund -- multi-day, high altitude)
- Long-distance cycling (100+ km in a day)
- Grade IV rafting
- Multi-day motorcycle tours on challenging terrain (Ladakh, Spiti)
- Full marathon and ultra-distance running
- Multi-pitch rock climbing

**Typical Profile**: Exercises 4-5 times per week. Has completed several moderate treks or equivalent activities. Understands their body's signals and limits.

## Expert
**Physical Capability**: Handles technical terrain, extreme weather, and high altitude (above 5,000 m) with confidence. Can make independent safety decisions.

**Suitable Activities**:
- Technical treks and mountaineering (Stok Kangri, Pin Parvati, Chadar)
- Grade V white water rafting and expedition kayaking
- Ultra-endurance cycling (200+ km Brevets, bikepacking)
- Extreme terrain motorcycle expeditions
- Ultra-marathons and multi-day trail races
- Lead climbing and alpine routes

**Typical Profile**: Years of outdoor experience. Holds relevant certifications (wilderness first aid, technical climbing). Physically trains for specific objectives. Can mentor others.

## Self-Assessment Checklist
Before selecting an activity difficulty, honestly answer:
- Can I match the distance and elevation stated in the event description?
- Have I done something similar before within the last 6 months?
- Am I comfortable with the altitude range mentioned?
- Do I have the required gear and know how to use it?
- If something goes wrong, can I handle it calmly?

**When in doubt, choose the easier option. You can always level up next time.**
