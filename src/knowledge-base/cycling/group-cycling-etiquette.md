# Group Cycling Etiquette

## The No-Drop Policy
A responsible group ride never leaves anyone behind. The group rides at the pace of the slowest member. If faster riders want to push ahead, they wait at designated regrouping points.

## Pace Lines and Drafting
Riding in a pace line reduces wind resistance by up to 30% for following riders.

**How It Works**:
- The front rider "pulls" for 1-2 minutes, then peels off to the back
- Maintain a consistent 30-50 cm gap from the wheel ahead (experienced riders only; beginners should keep 1 metre)
- Do NOT surge when you get to the front -- maintain the same speed
- Signal before pulling off: flick your elbow on the side you will peel off to

**Safety Rules**:
- Never half-wheel (overlap your front wheel with the rider ahead's rear wheel)
- Hold a straight line -- no sudden swerving
- Keep your hands near the brakes at all times
- Do not use aero bars in a group ride

## Calling Hazards
Clear verbal communication prevents crashes. Standard calls:
- **"Hole!"** or **"Glass!"** -- point at the hazard
- **"Car back!"** -- vehicle approaching from behind
- **"Car up!"** -- vehicle approaching from ahead (on narrow roads)
- **"Slowing!"** -- the group is reducing speed
- **"Stopping!"** -- coming to a complete stop
- **"Clear!"** or **"Not clear!"** -- at intersections
- **"On your left!"** -- passing another rider

Pass calls down the line -- every rider repeats the call for riders behind them.

## Waiting at Turns
- The lead rider stops at every junction and waits until all riders are visible
- At confusing turns, a rider stays at the junction to direct following riders
- Use GPS route sharing (Strava Live, Google Maps sharing) as backup navigation

## Mechanical Stops
- If a rider has a mechanical issue, at least one person stays to help
- The rest of the group waits at the next safe spot, not scattered along the road
- Carry a group repair kit: spare tubes, pump, multi-tool, chain quick links

## Ride Briefing Essentials
Before every group ride, discuss:
- Route and distance
- Average pace and regrouping points
- Water and food stop locations
- Emergency contact person not on the ride
- Hand signal and verbal call review for new members
