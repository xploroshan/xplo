# Hydration and Nutrition for Cyclists

## Water Intake Guidelines
Dehydration degrades performance and increases crash risk due to reduced concentration.

- **Before the ride**: Drink 500 ml of water 2 hours before starting
- **During the ride**: 150-250 ml every 15-20 minutes (one sip every few minutes)
- **Hot weather**: Increase intake by 50%. In Indian summers (35C+), you can lose 1-2 litres of sweat per hour
- **Rule of thumb**: If your urine is dark yellow, you are already dehydrated

## Electrolyte Management
Plain water is not enough for rides over 60 minutes. You lose sodium, potassium, and magnesium through sweat.

**Options available in India**:
- **ORS sachets** (Rs 5-10): Cheapest and most effective. Dissolve one sachet per 500 ml water
- **Electral powder**: Widely available at pharmacies
- **Fast&Up Reload** (Rs 25/tablet): Effervescent electrolyte tabs, convenient for bottles
- **Homemade**: 1 litre water + 6 teaspoons sugar + 1/2 teaspoon salt + juice of 1 lemon

## Energy and Meal Timing

### Pre-Ride (2-3 hours before)
Eat a carb-rich meal: oatmeal with banana, poha, idli-sambar, or peanut butter toast. Avoid heavy, oily food.

### During the Ride
- **Rides under 60 minutes**: Water only, no food needed
- **60-120 minutes**: One energy bar or banana per hour
- **120+ minutes**: 30-60g carbs per hour. Mix solid food (chikki, dates, paratha rolls) with energy gels or drinks

**Affordable Energy Foods**:
- Bananas (Rs 5 each) -- nature's energy bar
- Chikki / peanut bars (Rs 10-20) -- carbs + fat + protein
- Dates (3-4 pieces = quick sugar boost)
- Roasted chana -- slow-release energy
- Paratha with pickle -- perfect for long rides

### Post-Ride (within 30 minutes)
The recovery window is critical. Consume:
- Protein: 20-25g (glass of milk, paneer, eggs, or protein shake)
- Carbs: Replenish glycogen (rice, roti, fruit)
- Fluids: Replace 150% of weight lost during the ride

## Warning Signs of Dehydration
- Headache and dizziness
- Muscle cramps
- Nausea
- Reduced sweating despite heat
- **If you experience these, stop riding immediately, move to shade, and hydrate slowly**
