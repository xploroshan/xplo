# Vehicle Checklist for Road Trips

## Tyres
- **Tread depth**: Minimum 1.6 mm. Use a coin test -- insert a Rs 1 coin into the tread; if you can see the top of the lion capital, replace the tyre
- **Tyre pressure**: Check when cold (before driving). Refer to the sticker on the driver's door jamb
- **Spare tyre**: Confirm it is properly inflated and the jack and wheel spanner are present and functional
- **Visual inspection**: Look for bulges, cracks, or embedded objects on all four tyres plus the spare

## Fluids
- **Engine oil**: Check with dipstick. Level should be between min and max marks. Note the colour -- dark black means overdue for change
- **Coolant**: Check the overflow reservoir. Level should be between min and max when the engine is cold. Never open the radiator cap when hot
- **Brake fluid**: Check reservoir under the bonnet. Should be between min and max. If low, get brakes inspected
- **Windshield washer fluid**: Top up. Essential for dusty highways and monsoon driving
- **Power steering fluid** (if applicable): Check level in reservoir

## Brakes
- Test brakes before leaving -- the car should stop straight without pulling to one side
- Listen for squealing or grinding sounds (indicates worn pads)
- If the brake pedal feels spongy, get it inspected before travelling

## Lights and Electrics
- Headlights (low beam, high beam), tail lights, brake lights
- Turn indicators, hazard lights
- Reverse lights, fog lights (if equipped)
- Interior lights, dashboard warning lights (none should be illuminated)
- Horn -- must be functional
- Wiper blades -- replace if they streak or skip

## Documents (Mandatory to Carry)
- **Driving license** (original)
- **Vehicle RC** (Registration Certificate) -- original or DigiLocker copy
- **Insurance papers** -- valid and not expired (check Parivahan app)
- **PUC certificate** -- valid (typically 6 months for new vehicles, 1 year otherwise)
- **FASTag** -- sufficient balance for toll plazas
- If crossing state borders: no additional permits needed for private vehicles, but carry all originals

## Emergency Kit
- First aid kit
- Fire extinguisher (1 kg ABC type)
- Reflective warning triangles (2 minimum)
- Tow rope (3-tonne rated)
- Jumper cables
- Torch/flashlight with spare batteries
- Basic tool kit (screwdrivers, pliers, adjustable spanner)
- Duct tape and zip ties
