# Road Trip Emergency Kit

## First Aid Kit
Keep a well-stocked kit accessible (not buried in the boot):
- Adhesive bandages (assorted sizes), sterile gauze pads, medical tape
- Antiseptic wipes and povidone-iodine solution
- Crepe bandage for sprains (2 rolls)
- Triangular bandage (for sling)
- Paracetamol, ibuprofen, Disprin
- Antihistamine tablets (for allergic reactions)
- ORS sachets (minimum 5)
- Burnol or silver sulfadiazine cream
- Scissors, tweezers, safety pins
- Disposable gloves (4-5 pairs)
- Personal prescription medications (carry extra for delays)

## Vehicle Emergency Gear
- **Reflective warning triangles**: Place 50 metres behind your vehicle if stopped on the highway. Carry at least 2
- **Flashlight/torch**: LED, 500+ lumens, with fresh batteries. A headlamp is even better for hands-free use
- **Jumper cables**: 3-metre heavy-gauge cables. Learn how to jump-start before you need to
- **Tow rope**: Minimum 3-tonne rated, 4-5 metres long. Use shackle attachments, not knots
- **Basic tool kit**: Screwdrivers (flat + Phillips), pliers, adjustable spanner, Allen keys, wire cutter
- **Duct tape**: Fixes hoses, trim pieces, and temporary patches for almost anything
- **Zip ties**: Various sizes. Indispensable for temporary repairs
- **Tyre puncture kit**: Plug-type kit for tubeless tyres + portable air compressor (12V, plugs into cigarette lighter)

## Communication and Power
- **Phone charger**: Car charger + a 20,000 mAh power bank (fully charged before departure)
- **Spare charging cables**: At least two, different types if group has different phones
- Keep one phone with sufficient battery reserved for emergencies -- do not drain all devices on entertainment
- Download offline maps and important contacts before leaving network coverage

## Weather and Comfort
- **Umbrella**: For roadside tyre changes in rain or for sun protection
- **Blanket**: Thermal or fleece. Useful if stranded overnight or for injured passengers
- **Rain poncho**: Lightweight, foldable. For roadside emergencies in rain
- **Sunglasses**: Reduce glare fatigue on long drives
- **Water**: Carry minimum 5 litres per person. Refill at every major stop

## Documents and Cash
- Photocopies of all vehicle documents (separate from originals)
- Emergency contact list printed on paper (phones can die)
- Minimum Rs 2,000-5,000 cash for areas without digital payment
- Breakdown assistance number for your car brand or insurance roadside assist number

## Roadside Assistance Numbers
- Your car manufacturer's RSA (Roadside Assistance) number
- Insurance company helpline
- National Highway helpline: 1033
- National emergency: 112
