# Route Planning for Road Trips

## Map Preparation
- **Download offline maps** before departure. In Google Maps: search for the region, tap "Download" on the area. Download covers a 50 km x 50 km area per tile
- **Alternative apps**: OsmAnd (fully offline, detailed Indian maps), MapmyIndia (now Mappls -- best for Indian roads)
- Mark key waypoints: fuel stations, toll plazas, hospitals, and planned rest stops
- Check for road closures, landslides (common in hill areas), or construction on your route

## Fuel Station Planning
- **Never let your tank drop below quarter** -- fuel stations in remote areas may be closed, out of fuel, or accept only cash
- Research fuel availability on your route using the IOCL/BPCL/HPCL station locator apps
- In the Himalayas, Rajasthan desert, or Northeast India, fuel stations can be 80-150 km apart
- Carry a 5-litre jerry can for remote stretches (ensure proper ventilation in the vehicle)
- Note which stations accept digital payments vs cash only

## Driving Schedule
- **Maximum 8-10 hours of driving per day** -- fatigue is the leading cause of highway accidents in India
- Take a **15-minute break every 2 hours**. Get out, stretch, walk around
- **Avoid night driving** (8 PM to 6 AM) wherever possible. Reasons: unlit vehicles, animals on roads, blinding high beams, drunk drivers, poor road markings
- Start early (5-6 AM) to cover maximum distance in daylight

## Rest Stop Intervals
- Plan stops every 150-200 km or every 2 hours, whichever comes first
- Identify highway dhabas and restaurants with parking and clean washrooms
- On major highways (NH44, NH48, NH8): Reliance/Adani rest areas offer fuel, food, and washrooms
- Carry snacks and water for stretches where facilities are sparse

## Highway Tips for India
- **Toll plazas**: Keep FASTag active. Fast lane is the leftmost lane in most plazas
- **Overtaking**: Only from the right on divided highways. Flash headlights to signal intent
- **Speed limits**: 100-120 km/h on expressways, 80-100 on national highways, 40-50 in cities. Speed cameras are increasingly common
- **Animals on roads**: Common in rural stretches. Reduce speed near villages, especially at dawn and dusk
- Share live location with family through Google Maps or WhatsApp for the entire trip duration
