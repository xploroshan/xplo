# Hydration Guide for Runners

## Pre-Run Hydration
Good hydration starts hours before the run, not at the starting line.

- **Night before**: Drink 500 ml water with dinner. Avoid alcohol
- **2 hours before**: Drink 400-500 ml water or electrolyte drink
- **30 minutes before**: Small sips only (100-150 ml). Do not chug water -- it causes sloshing and nausea
- **Urine check**: Pale yellow = properly hydrated. Dark yellow = dehydrated. Clear = possibly over-hydrated

## During the Run

### Short Runs (Under 60 minutes)
- Water only is sufficient
- Sip 100-150 ml every 15-20 minutes if available
- In Indian heat (30C+), drink more frequently

### Long Runs (60+ minutes)
- Alternate between water and electrolyte drink
- **Target**: 150-250 ml every 15-20 minutes
- Practice your hydration strategy during training -- never try something new on race day

## Electrolyte Timing
You lose 1-2 grams of sodium per litre of sweat. Plain water alone will not replace this.

- **Start electrolytes from the 45-minute mark** on runs over 60 minutes
- **Options**: ORS sachets, Electral, Fast&Up, or homemade mix (see below)
- **Homemade electrolyte drink**: 500 ml water + 1/4 teaspoon salt + 2 tablespoons honey + splash of lemon juice
- **Warning**: Hyponatremia (low sodium from drinking too much plain water) is as dangerous as dehydration. If you feel bloated and confused after drinking lots of water, stop drinking and seek medical help

## Race Day Aid Station Strategy
- **Know the course**: Check where aid stations are located (typically every 2-3 km in Indian races)
- **Slow down slightly** when grabbing cups -- spilling wastes more than the 2 seconds you save
- **Pinch the cup** at the top to create a spout for easier drinking while moving
- Alternate water and electrolyte stations if both are offered
- Do not skip stations in hot weather -- even if you do not feel thirsty

## Weather-Based Adjustments

### Hot Weather (30C+, Common in Most Indian Cities)
- Increase fluid intake by 50%
- Pre-cool with ice water or ice towel on neck
- Pour water over your head at aid stations to cool down
- Wear light-coloured, moisture-wicking clothing
- Consider starting earlier (5-6 AM) to avoid peak heat

### Humid Weather (Monsoon Season)
- Sweat does not evaporate effectively -- you overheat faster
- Drink more electrolytes, not just water
- Reduce pace expectations by 10-15%
- Watch for signs of heat exhaustion: dizziness, nausea, goosebumps, stopping sweating

### Cool Weather (Winter Races)
- You still sweat and need fluids -- do not skip hydration because you feel cold
- Warm fluids are more palatable and help maintain core temperature
- Reduce intake slightly compared to summer (about 20% less)
