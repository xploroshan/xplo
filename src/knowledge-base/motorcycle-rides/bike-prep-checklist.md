# Pre-Ride Bike Preparation Checklist

Complete this checklist **before every ride**, especially before long-distance or group rides.

## Tyres
- Check tyre pressure (refer to your owner's manual -- typically 28-32 PSI front, 32-36 PSI rear for most bikes)
- Inspect tread depth -- minimum 1.5 mm remaining
- Look for cuts, cracks, embedded objects, or sidewall bulges
- Carry a **puncture repair kit** and a portable air compressor

## Brakes
- Squeeze front and rear brakes individually -- they should feel firm, not spongy
- Check brake pad thickness -- replace if less than 2 mm
- Inspect brake fluid level -- top up if below minimum mark
- Test ABS if equipped (gentle brake on gravel at low speed)

## Chain and Drivetrain
- Chain slack: 20-30 mm of play (check your manual for exact spec)
- Lubricate chain with a quality chain lube (Motul C2, Ipone chain lube)
- Inspect sprockets for hooked or worn teeth
- For shaft-drive bikes: check for leaks

## Lights and Electrics
- Headlight (low and high beam), tail light, brake light
- Turn indicators -- all four
- Horn -- must be loud and functional
- Instrument cluster -- fuel gauge, temperature, warning lights

## Fluids
- Engine oil level -- between min and max on dipstick
- Coolant level (liquid-cooled bikes) -- top up with manufacturer-recommended coolant
- Clutch and brake fluid reservoirs

## Essential Toolkit
Pack these on every ride:
- Puncture kit + portable compressor
- Basic spanners (8mm, 10mm, 12mm, 14mm)
- Screwdriver set (flat + Phillips)
- Zip ties and electrical tape
- Spare clutch and brake levers (for long tours)
- Chain lube
- Fuse set matching your bike's specs

## Documents
- Driving license
- Vehicle RC (Registration Certificate)
- Insurance papers (valid and not expired)
- PUC (Pollution Under Control) certificate
- Emergency contact card in your jacket pocket
