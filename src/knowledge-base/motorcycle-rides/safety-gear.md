# Essential Safety Gear for Motorcycle Rides

## Helmet (Non-Negotiable)
Always wear an **ISI-certified (IS 4151)** or **ECE 22.06** certified helmet. Popular options in India:
- **Budget**: Steelbird SBA-7 (~Rs 2,000), Studds Thunder (~Rs 1,500)
- **Mid-range**: MT Thunder 3 (~Rs 4,500), Axor Apex (~Rs 5,000)
- **Premium**: SMK Stellar (~Rs 7,000), LS2 FF320 (~Rs 8,000)

Full-face helmets offer maximum protection. Half-face helmets are NOT recommended for highway or group rides.

## Riding Jacket
A proper jacket protects against abrasion and impact. Look for CE-rated armour at shoulders, elbows, and back.
- **Rynox Air GT 4** (~Rs 5,500) - best for hot Indian summers, excellent ventilation
- **Royal Enfield Explorer V2** (~Rs 6,000) - good all-rounder
- **Solace Rival Urban** (~Rs 4,500) - budget-friendly with decent protection

## Gloves
Riding gloves protect your hands in slides and improve grip. Choose knuckle-armoured gloves with palm sliders.
- **Rynox Tornado Pro 3** (~Rs 1,800) - great value
- **Viaterra Holeshot** (~Rs 2,500) - premium feel

## Riding Boots
Ankle injuries are among the most common in motorcycle crashes. Wear boots that cover the ankle with a reinforced sole.
- **Royal Enfield Jackboot** (~Rs 4,500)
- **Solace CE-rated boots** (~Rs 3,500)

## Knee Guards
Essential for rides without armoured riding pants.
- **Rynox Basalt Knee Guards** (~Rs 1,200) - CE Level 2 rated
- **Biking Brotherhood knee armour** (~Rs 900)

## Quick Rules
- Never ride without at least a helmet, gloves, and sturdy footwear
- Replace your helmet after any crash or after 5 years of use
- Wear bright or reflective gear for night rides
- Check that all armour fits snugly -- loose armour shifts on impact
