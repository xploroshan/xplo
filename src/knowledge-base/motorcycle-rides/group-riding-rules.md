# Group Riding Rules and Etiquette

## Formation
Ride in a **staggered formation** on highways -- lead rider on the right, second rider on the left, offset by 1-2 seconds. This gives each rider maximum visibility and braking room. On narrow or winding roads, switch to single file.

## Roles
- **Lead Rider**: Sets the pace, navigates. Must be experienced and know the route.
- **Sweep Rider**: Rides last. Carries first aid and tools. Never passes anyone. Ensures no one is left behind.
- **All Riders**: Maintain formation position. Do not swap positions mid-ride unless signalled.

## Hand Signals (Memorize These)
- **Left turn**: Left arm extended straight out
- **Right turn**: Left arm bent upward at 90 degrees
- **Slow down**: Left arm extended downward, palm facing back
- **Stop**: Left arm extended downward, fist clenched
- **Hazard left/right**: Point at the ground on the hazard side
- **Single file**: Left index finger raised above helmet
- **Fuel stop**: Point to tank with left hand

## Communication
Bluetooth intercoms make group rides safer. Recommended:
- **Cardo Packtalk Edge** - premium, mesh network up to 15 riders
- **Sena 50R** - reliable, up to 2 km range
- **Budget**: Ejeas V6 Pro (~Rs 3,000) - supports 6 riders

## Absolute Rules
1. **No overtaking within the group** -- ever
2. Maintain a minimum 2-second gap to the rider ahead
3. Ride at the pace of the slowest rider
4. Pull over safely if you need to stop -- signal first
5. Never ride fatigued -- call for a break when needed
6. Pre-ride briefing is mandatory: discuss route, fuel stops, emergency plan
7. Share your live location with at least one person not on the ride

## Fuel and Rest Stops
Plan fuel stops before anyone hits reserve. Take a 10-15 minute break every 90-120 km on highway rides.
