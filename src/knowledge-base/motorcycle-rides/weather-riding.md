# Riding in Different Weather Conditions

## Monsoon Riding (June - September)
The most dangerous season for motorcyclists in India. Roads are slippery, visibility drops, and potholes hide under water.

**Essential Gear**:
- Full waterproof riding suit or rain oversuit (Rynox H2Go, Royal Enfield rain liner)
- Waterproof gloves or latex glove liners
- Anti-fog visor insert (Pinlock recommended)
- Waterproof boot covers or riding boots with waterproof membrane

**Tips**:
- Reduce speed by at least 30% on wet roads
- Avoid painted road markings, manhole covers, and metal surfaces -- extremely slippery when wet
- Increase following distance to 4-5 seconds
- Do NOT ride through standing water deeper than your wheel hub
- Use low beam in heavy rain -- high beam reflects off raindrops and blinds you
- Brake earlier and more gently -- wet brakes need longer to engage

## Winter Riding (November - February)
Fog is the primary killer in North India. Hypothermia is a real risk at higher altitudes.

**Gear**:
- Thermal base layer (merino wool or synthetic)
- Heated grips or handlebar muffs (common for RE riders)
- Balaclava and neck warmer
- Anti-fog visor

**Tips**:
- Avoid riding between 10 PM and 7 AM during fog season
- Use fog lights if equipped -- not high beam
- Wear high-visibility reflective vest
- Watch for black ice above 2,000 m elevation

## Summer Riding (March - May)
Heat exhaustion is the main risk. Temperatures regularly exceed 40C in plains.

**Hydration**:
- Drink 200-300 ml water every 30 minutes
- Use a hydration pack (CamelBak or similar)
- Carry ORS sachets

**Tips**:
- Wear a ventilated riding jacket -- never ride without one just because it is hot
- Take shade breaks every 60-90 minutes
- Start rides early morning (5-6 AM) to avoid peak heat
- Watch for tar bleeding on roads -- creates slippery patches
- Use a wet bandana under your helmet for cooling
