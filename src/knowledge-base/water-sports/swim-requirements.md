# Swimming Requirements for Water Sports

## Why Swimming Proficiency Matters
Even with life jackets, swimming ability dramatically increases your survival chances if you fall into water. Every participant must honestly assess their swimming level before joining water activities.

## Minimum Requirements by Activity

### White Water Rafting (Grade I-III)
- **Minimum**: Ability to float on your back for 2 minutes and swim 50 metres in calm water
- **Life jacket**: Mandatory (provided by operator)
- **Note**: You will likely fall in. Being comfortable in moving water is essential

### White Water Rafting (Grade IV-V)
- **Minimum**: Strong swimmer -- 200 metres freestyle in open water
- **Must know**: Defensive swimming position (on back, feet downstream, toes up)
- **Life jacket**: Mandatory

### Kayaking (Flatwater/Sea)
- **Minimum**: Swim 100 metres comfortably, tread water for 5 minutes
- **Must know**: How to wet exit from a capsized kayak
- **PFD**: Mandatory at all times

### Stand-Up Paddleboarding
- **Minimum**: Swim 50 metres, comfortable in water above your head
- **Leash**: Always attached to ankle or calf
- **PFD**: Recommended, mandatory for children

### Surfing
- **Minimum**: Swim 200 metres in the ocean, comfortable in waves and currents
- **Must know**: How to handle rip currents (swim parallel to shore, not against the current)

### Scuba Diving
- **Minimum**: Swim 200 metres without stopping, float/tread water for 10 minutes
- **Certification**: PADI Open Water or SSI equivalent required for non-introductory dives

## Life Jacket / PFD Types
- **Type I (Offshore)**: Maximum buoyancy (22+ lbs). For rough, open water
- **Type III (Flotation Aid)**: Most common for water sports. Comfortable, allows movement. 15+ lbs buoyancy
- **Type V (Special Use)**: Activity-specific (whitewater PFDs with higher flotation and impact protection)

## Fit Check
- A properly fitted PFD should be snug but allow breathing
- Pull the shoulder straps up -- the jacket should not ride above your chin
- All buckles and zippers must be fastened
- Children must wear child-sized PFDs, never adult sizes
- **Never substitute inflatable pool toys for proper PFDs**
