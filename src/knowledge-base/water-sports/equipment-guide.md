# Water Sports Equipment Guide

## What Operators Should Provide
Reputable operators in India should provide all essential safety and activity gear. If they do not, reconsider booking with them.

### Rafting
**Operator provides**: Raft, paddles, helmets, PFDs (life jackets), throw bags, first aid kit, safety kayaker
**You bring**: Quick-dry clothing, water shoes with grip (not flip-flops), sunscreen, eyewear retainer strap

### Kayaking
**Operator provides**: Kayak, paddle, PFD, spray skirt (for sit-in kayaks), safety briefing
**You bring**: Quick-dry clothing, sun hat with chin strap, water bottle, dry bag for phone/wallet

### Surfing
**Operator provides**: Surfboard, leash, rash guard (sometimes)
**You bring**: Swimwear, reef-safe sunscreen, water shoes for rocky beaches, towel

### Stand-Up Paddleboarding
**Operator provides**: Board, paddle, leash, PFD (should provide, often does not -- ask)
**You bring**: Swimwear, sunscreen, waterproof phone pouch, hat with chin strap

## Gear You Should Own (If You Do This Regularly)

### Essentials
- **Water shoes**: Protect feet from rocks and coral. Wildcraft or Decathlon aqua shoes (Rs 500-1,500)
- **Quick-dry shorts and shirt**: Avoid cotton -- it stays wet and causes chafing
- **Rash guard**: Protects against sunburn and abrasion. Full-sleeve recommended for rivers
- **Dry bag**: 10-20 litre waterproof bag for valuables. Starts at Rs 500 on Amazon
- **Waterproof phone pouch**: Universal type with lanyard (Rs 200-500)
- **Polarised sunglasses with retainer strap**: Reduce water surface glare

### Nice to Have
- **Neoprene gloves**: Prevent paddle blisters on multi-day trips
- **Wetsuit** (2-3 mm): For cold water -- Himalayan river rafting in early season or Goa winter surfing
- **Action camera with mount**: GoPro or budget alternatives (SJCAM, Akaso) with chest or head mount
- **Waterproof headlamp**: For pre-dawn or evening activities

## Red Flags When Choosing an Operator
- PFDs are old, torn, or buckles do not work
- No safety briefing before the activity
- No trained rescue personnel or safety kayaker on rafting trips
- Equipment looks damaged or poorly maintained
- They do not ask about your swimming ability
- No first aid kit visible
