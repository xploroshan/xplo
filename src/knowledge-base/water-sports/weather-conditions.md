# Weather and Water Conditions for Water Sports

## Wind Speed Guidelines
Wind dramatically affects water sports safety. Check forecasts before heading out.

| Wind Speed | Conditions | Suitable For |
|-----------|------------|-------------|
| 0-10 km/h | Calm | All activities, ideal for beginners |
| 10-20 km/h | Light breeze | Experienced paddlers, good for sailing/kiting |
| 20-30 km/h | Moderate | Only experienced surfers/kiters. No beginners on water |
| 30+ km/h | Strong | Cancel all recreational water activities |

## Wave Heights
- **0-0.5 m**: Suitable for SUP, kayaking, beginner surfing
- **0.5-1.5 m**: Intermediate surfing, experienced kayakers
- **1.5-3 m**: Advanced surfers only
- **3+ m**: Expert only. Most operators will cancel

## River Conditions

### Understanding River Grades
- **Grade I**: Easy. Gentle current, small ripples. Beginners welcome
- **Grade II**: Moderate. Straightforward rapids, wide channels. Minimal manoeuvring needed
- **Grade III**: Difficult. Irregular waves, strong currents. Swimming ability mandatory
- **Grade IV**: Very difficult. Powerful rapids, precise manoeuvring required. Only experienced rafters
- **Grade V**: Extremely difficult. Violent rapids, serious consequences for errors. Expert only

### Monsoon River Levels (June-September)
Rivers across India swell dramatically during monsoon. Critical considerations:
- Water levels can rise **several metres within hours** after heavy rain upstream
- River colour turns muddy brown -- reduced visibility means hidden obstacles
- Debris (logs, branches) in the water increases collision risk
- Most rafting operators in Rishikesh, Kolad, and Coorg **suspend operations** during peak monsoon
- Post-monsoon (October-November) often has the best rafting conditions -- high water but clearer

## Coastal Tide Charts
- Always check tide times before beach water sports
- **Incoming tide**: Water level rises, currents intensify, beach shrinks
- **Outgoing tide**: Can create strong rip currents pulling you out to sea
- Best time for most beach activities: **2 hours either side of low tide**
- Check tide charts at tide-forecast.com or IndianTides app

## Current Awareness
- **Rip currents**: Fast, narrow channels of water flowing away from shore. If caught, swim parallel to shore until out of the current, then swim back
- **Lateral currents**: Can sweep you sideways along the coast. Stay aware of your position relative to shore landmarks
- **River hydraulics**: Recirculating currents below waterfalls and weirs can trap swimmers. Stay well clear of these features

## When to Stay Off the Water
- Thunderstorms forecasted within 3 hours
- Visibility below 500 metres (fog, heavy rain)
- River in flood or rising rapidly
- Official warnings from coast guard, local authorities, or operators
- You feel unwell, fatigued, or have consumed alcohol
